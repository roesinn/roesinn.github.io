The benchmarking process abstractly consists of three levels: Setup, Execution and Analysis.

  1. The Setup defines the design of a benchmark experiment; data set, candidate algorithms, performance measures and a suitable resampling strategy are declared.
  2. In the Execution level the defined setup is executed. Here, computational aspects play a major role; an important example is the parallel computation of the experiment on different computers.
  3. In the Analysis level the computed raw performance measures are analyzed using exploratory and inferential methods. This package is mainly concerned with the Analysis level; in what the derivation of a statistically correct order of the candidate algorithms is a major objective.
